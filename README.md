Maternal and Child Health Analysis Dashboard — Nigeria

📌 Project Overview

This project presents an in-depth analysis of maternal and child health indicators across Nigeria, using survey data covering 154 Antenatal Care (ANC) visits. The goal was to uncover patterns in healthcare access, identify regional and socioeconomic disparities, and track progress in key health outcomes such as maternal mortality, under-5 mortality, malnutrition, and vaccination coverage.


The analysis is presented through two interactive dashboards:

Key Maternal and Child Health Indicators Dashboard

Sociodemographic Data of Respondents Dashboard



🛠️ Tools & Technologies


Microsoft Excel — Data cleaning, structuring, and preliminary analysis

Microsoft Power BI — Data modeling, DAX calculations, and interactive dashboard visualization



📊 Dashboard 1: Key Indicators for Maternal and Child Health

This dashboard tracks core health outcomes over time and across demographic groups.

Key Metrics:

ANC Visits: 154

Malaria in Pregnancy Cases: 75

Wasting Cases: 76

Stunting Cases: 56

Insights:


Fully vaccinated children declined from 37 (2022) to 19 (2024).

Maternal deaths dropped from 20 (2023) to 9 (2024).

Under-5 mortality fell from 22 (2022) to 9 (2024), signaling improving outcomes.

Poor households recorded the highest Under-5 mortality (20 cases) by wealth quintile, compared to 16 (Middle) and 9 (Rich).

Rural and Semi-Urban areas had the highest number of skilled birth attendants (31 and 30 respectively).

📊 Dashboard 2: Sociodemographic Data of Respondents

This dashboard explores who is accessing care, where, and how that varies by region, education, and facility type.

Key Metrics:

Total ANC Visits: 154

Health Insurance: NHIS (91) vs Out of Pocket (63)

ANC Visits by Year: 2022 (67) → 2023 (53) → 2024 (34)

Insights:

North-West geopolitical zone recorded the highest ANC visits (43), while South-East recorded the lowest (15).

Primary Health Centers were the most utilized facility type (37 visits).

Rural residents recorded the most ANC visits (56), followed closely by Semi-Urban and Urban (49 each).

ANC visits declined year-over-year, dropping from 67 (2022) to 34 (2024) — a trend that requires urgent attention.

Oyo State led all states with 13 ANC visits, while several northern states (Zamfara, Anambra, Enugu) recorded the fewest (4 each).

Exclusive breastfeeding rates remained fairly consistent across education levels (18–23 cases).

🔍 Key Findings


✅ Positive Progress: Significant reductions in maternal and under-5 mortality over the analysis period.

⚠️ Concerning Trend: A steady decline in ANC visit turnout from 2022 to 2024.

⚠️ Equity Gap: Wealthier households, counterintuitively, recorded higher under-5 mortality rates than poorer households.

⚠️ Coverage Gap: A notable share of respondents remain uninsured (Out of Pocket), and vaccination coverage is declining.

💡 Recommendations
Investigate the causes behind declining ANC turnout and vaccination rates.

Strengthen Primary Health Center capacity given their central role in care delivery.

Conduct further research into the unexpected under-5 mortality pattern among wealthier households.

Expand health insurance enrollment, particularly in underserved geopolitical zones.
